Nothing is Impossible...

During his time as the 3D Design Manager for Scenario Design, Inc., he was involved in building the design and engineering team that was the first to apply BIM/VDC to large scale entertainment projects for major theme parks in the U.S. and Asia. However, due to the complexity of the projects and the diversity of software involved we quickly realized that existing project and information management systems were ill-equipped to handle the vast amounts of data that BIM/VDC generated. In addition to this new type of project data, the complex relationships between the many stakeholders of a project, geographic locations, and varied processes made it difficult to track and maintain the information, activities, and transactions between all the stakeholders. This need led to the concept of a unified project information management system that integrated people, processes, software, and information into one centralized resource. Realizing that this solution could be applied to the entire AEC industry and not just our specialized sector, Paolo Hilario, Eric Boer, and David Moyer began the 8 month process of Architecting the System and developing the initial prototype for VPD.

KHS&S Contractors later acquired Scenario Design, Inc. for our advancements in rockwork technology and this burgeoning concept of this "Virtual Project Delivery" system was supported by their progressive and forward thinking executive leadership. With a small team of 7 programmers and 2 SQA staff we proceeded to tackle the impossible task of building a mammoth enterprise level system in less than a year and a half. The Scenario VPD team launched the commercial offering of our C3 (Collaboration, Communication, Control) product, to the AEC industry in Q1 of 2011.

Technology... Meet Process...

Also during his time as the 3D Design Manager of Scenario Design, Inc., he had the opportunity to work with a talented team of engineers, designers, and construction professionals to develop both the rockwork design process and the software Wireworks 3D which is now being used on the largest rockwork project ever constructed as well as other major rockwork projects in theme parks around the world. Our goal was to automate the processing of high resolution scans of conceptual foam models provided to us by our clientsâ€™ creative teams to generate assembly drawings and CNC bar bend data.

The current industry processes required large design staff to manually generate each drawing and output the CNC data. We were able to reduce what would normally take 18,000 man-hours of design time down to nearly 4,000 man-hours. In addition to these advancements in design, our manufacturing engineers devised an efficient rockwork production assembly line which made the fabrication and assembly of prototypical rebar and lathe panels as efficient as building widgets. This combination of software technology and lean manufacturing processes resulted in unprecedented control, consistency, and efficiency in a historically manual and unpredictable area of themed construction.
